Brown's Scili Printer


Brown's Printing System

Lets users print whatever and however many copies they want.

The key components consist of uploading on myprint.brown.edu and then printing on the print release screen.



Observations
Most users did not have trouble and were not bothered while using the interface. General patterns include users waiting for the paper to print: tapping their feet, organizing sheets of paper, and/or on their phone.

New users however, had trouble and were confused. Brown's printing system instructions were not in plain view and going on myprint.brown.edu, the first step, was not intuitive. New users took a lot of time and were confused throughout the process.

Interview Summary
The most important things for a printing system is for it to be fast, simple, accessible, color, and cheap.

Brown's printing system has a high learning curve and is not intuitive for new users, but has decent memorability and good efficiency

The first half, going on myprint.brown.edu, was not intuitive and full of statistics and jargon. The upload/submit job button was not in plain view.

The second half, the print release station was intuitive, in plain view, but was misleading- it seemed like the first step instead of being the last.

Brown's printing system was used mostly for classes and extracurriculars printing things such as sheet music, kanji sheets, study guides, posters, and papers.
